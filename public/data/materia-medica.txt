Aconitum napellus
Fear, anxiety, and restlessness are the main characteristics of Aconite. It is indicated in acute, sudden, and violent inflammations with high fever. The patient does not want to be touched. There is a sudden and great sinking of strength. Complaints are often caused by exposure to dry, cold weather.

Mind: Great fear and anxiety. Fear of death. Predicts the day he will die. Restlessness.

Head: Fullness and heaviness, as if the head would burst.

Eyes: Red, inflamed, and sensitive to light.

Ears: Very sensitive to noises.

Nose: Smell is acutely sensitive. Coryza with much sneezing.

Face: Red, hot, and flushed.

Mouth: Dry and thirsty for cold water.

Stomach: Vomiting with fear and heat.

Abdomen: Hot, tense, and sensitive to touch.

Stool: Watery and white.

Urine: Scanty, red, and hot.

Respiratory: Dry, hoarse, croupy cough.

Heart: Palpitation with anxiety and fainting.

Extremities: Numbness and tingling.

Fever: High fever with dry, hot skin and restlessness.

Modalities: Worse in a warm room, in the evening, and at night. Better in the open air.

Belladonna
Great intensity of symptoms. Sudden and violent onset. The patient is hot, red, and burning. The pains are throbbing. There is a great deal of delirium, with visions and a desire to escape.

Mind: Delirium with wild imaginings. Wants to bite, strike, and tear things.

Head: Throbbing headache, worse from light, noise, and motion.

Eyes: Pupils are dilated. Eyes are red, hot, and sparkling.

Face: Red, hot, and swollen.

Throat: Dry, red, and constricted.

Stomach: Great thirst for cold water, but dreads drinking.

Abdomen: Tender and distended.

Fever: High fever with a hot, red skin, and a bounding pulse.

Modalities: Worse from touch, motion, noise, and light. Better from rest.
